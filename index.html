About this chart: The "happy hormones" (often called DOSE) are a popular framework, not a precise science. The curves here are illustrative — hand-tuned shapes, not measured data — meant to convey one idea: effortful, healthy habits tend to raise these chemicals gradually and sustainably, while quick chemical shortcuts (junk food, gambling, drugs, alcohol, chasing validation) tend to spike them and then leave you below baseline. Real neurochemistry is far messier, overlapping, and person-specific. The items in the red group are flagged as things to avoid, not recommendations, and nothing here is medical advice.
